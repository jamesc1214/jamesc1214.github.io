Reading Response Week 3:

In her article, Nesrine Malik warns how “AI slop” is slowly changing the internet and the way people see reality. After reading her article, I felt that her message was very true because of the amount of AI-generated content I’ve seen online. For me, it’s been hard to take time to determine if a picture, video, or article is real or fake. This makes me worried about how easily people can be misled in the future.

One idea that stood out to me was how AI is replacing human creativity. AI can make stories, pictures, and videos in seconds but often feel empty and repetitive. I think human creativity is special because it comes from real life experiences and emotions. When I read something that is written by a real person, I usually feel more connected to the meaning behind it. If the internet becomes just AI-made content, it could lose the human feeling.

I also agree with Malik’s point about misinformation. Fake news and deepfakes can spread very quickly especially on social media. I’ve seen examples of edited videos and fake images that confuse many people. If AI keeps improving, it may be even harder to know what to trust. This could create fear and division because people might stop believing reliable sources.

Another issue Malik raises is the effects on artists, writers, and even creative people. AI systems are trained using human work but many creators don’t get credit or payment for it. Some people may even lose their jobs due to AI because companies may choose cheaper AI content instead of human work. Malik believes that this could reduce the value of human creativity and imagination.

Overall, I found the article thoughtful and important. Malik’s warning about “sleepwalking into disaster” feels realistic because many people are excited about what AI can do without considering the consequences. In some ways, AI can prove to be useful but society needs to be careful and responsible. Malik encourages readers to think carefully about how AI is changing the world and why it’s important to protect human creativity.
